Panther Design System — Complete Specification

Version 1.0 — Working Name: PANTHER

---

1. Executive Summary & Session Context

This document codifies the entire visual, spatial, tonal, and interaction design system developed through a rigorous, iterative session between the brand founder and an AI reasoning model. The goal was to create a distinctive, trust‑anchored identity for a used‑car marketplace operating in Nigeria and expanding across Africa, targeting a 25‑35 year old digitally native audience.

The system is built around a single mascot – the Black Panther (melanistic leopard) – which was chosen for its deep aspirational and cultural resonance, its duality (guardian vs. predator), and its ability to be sculpted into four distinct postures that map directly to user journey stages. Every design decision (colour, typography, layout, motion, copy) is derived from these postures, creating a cohesive, emotionally intelligent brand language.

---

2. The Panther Mascot – Foundation

2.1 Taxonomic Clarification

The "Black Panther" is not a separate species; it is a melanistic colour variant found primarily in leopards (Panthera pardus) in Africa/Asia and jaguars (Panthera onca) in the Americas. For this brand, we anchor to the African leopard melanistic phenotype, associating it with the continent’s royal and protective symbolism.

2.2 Why the Panther?

· Aspirational weight – the “unreachable” mystique that luxury and performance brands covet.
· Cultural resonance – in many African traditions, the leopard/panther symbolises kingship, spiritual power, and guardianship.
· Marvel/Wakanda effect – a generational association with hidden, technologically advanced African excellence.
· Duality – it can be framed as a guardian (trust) or predator (risk) – we deliberately choose the guardian frame, but the duality allows flexibility.

2.3 The Four Postures (Locked)

We rejected pouncing and stalking (aggressive, hidden). We selected four postures, each with a distinct emotional payload and user action:

Posture Emotional State User Action Geometric Bias
Roaring Calm Sovereign Confidence Enter & Discover (top‑funnel) Wide, triangular, expansive
Walking Forward Guided Curiosity Explore & Compare (mid‑funnel) Diagonal, directional
Eyes-Forward Focused Scrutiny Inspect & Validate (analytical) Vertical, frontal, symmetrical
Resting Assured Calm Confirm & Commit (terminal) Horizontal, low centre of gravity

These postures are non‑negotiable and serve as the primary axis for all subsequent design decisions.

3. Colour System – Perceptual Physics

3.1 Core Principles

· Colours are modulators, not labels – they amplify or attenuate the geometric bias of each posture.
· We use OKLCH colour space for perceptual uniformity across devices; HEX is provided for legacy fallback.
· The Lightness axis is the primary differentiator between Resting and Walking Forward (Option A locked).
· Saturation and Temperature are secondary, but fixed per posture.

3.2 Locked Colour Tokens

Posture OKLCH Value sRGB Hex Lightness (L) Chroma (C) Hue (H)
Resting oklch(20% 0.02 30) #34312E 0.20 (±0.02) 0.02 30° (neutral‑warm)
Walking Forward oklch(62% 0.08 210) #9BAEBF 0.62 (±0.03) 0.08 210° (slight cool)
Eyes-Forward oklch(45% 0.03 225) #6F767D 0.45 (±0.02) 0.03 225° (pronounced cool)
Roaring Calm oklch(50% 0.18 30) #CB823F 0.50 (±0.02) 0.18 30° (pronounced warm)

3.3 Enforceable Constraints

· Resting Lightness must be at least 30 points lower than Walking Forward Lightness (0.20 vs 0.62 – delta 0.42) to survive display variability.
· Walking Forward Chroma must remain between 0.07 and 0.09.
· Eyes‑Forward Chroma must not exceed 0.035 (functionally achromatic).
· Roaring Calm Hue must be within 28°–32°.
· Resting never appears on dark backgrounds (fails contrast); it must only be used on light backgrounds (≥ 70% lightness).

4. Typography – Dual‑Font System (Option B)

We implement a two‑font architecture: a serif for authority/gravity (Roaring Calm + Resting) and a sans‑serif for motion/clarity (Walking Forward + Eyes‑Forward). This gives us morphological distinctness that colour alone cannot achieve.

4.1 Serif Track – GT Alpina (Primary)

Mandatory Criteria (locked):

· Morphology: Unbracketed, sharp geometric serifs (reject old‑style).
· Stroke Contrast: High (≥ 3:1 thick‑to‑thin).
· Weight: Variable or multi‑weight – must support Heavy/Black for headlines.
· X‑Height: Moderate (0.48–0.52 relative to cap height).
· Width: Normal to expanded.
· Curve Contrast: Minimal bracketing.

Usage:

· Roaring Calm: headlines, manifestos, hero copy (full weight range).
· Resting: contract text, final confirmation pages (only Bold/Black – never Light).

Backups (free, SIL OFL):

1. Alike Angular – sharp, geometric, moderate‑high contrast, large x‑height.
2. Prata – didone with sharp triangular serifs, high contrast, Google Fonts.

4.2 Sans‑Serif Track – Inter (Primary)

Mandatory Criteria:

· Morphology: Geometric or neo‑grotesque (reject humanist).
· Stroke Contrast: Low (≤ 1.2:1).
· Weight: Variable or multi‑weight – Thin (100) to Bold (700) required.
· X‑Height: Large (≥ 0.54, ≤ 0.60).
· Width: Slightly condensed (≤ 95%) for Walking Forward; normal for Eyes‑Forward.
· Aperture: Open (distinguishable a, c, e).

Usage:

· Walking Forward: body text, filter labels, car names (Regular 400, Medium 500).
· Eyes‑Forward: VIN numbers, inspection data (Light 300 for data, Bold 700 for badges).

Backup: None required – Inter is open‑source and universally available.

4.3 Cross‑System Harmony Rules

· Cap‑height alignment: both families must have cap‑heights within ±2% at the same declared font‑size.
· The serif must never be used with Eyes‑Forward colour (#6F767D) – that pair is forbidden.
· The sans‑serif must never be used with Resting colour (#34312E) – that pair is forbidden.
· Enforcement via CSS utility classes: .serif-track and .sans-track with strict linting.

5. Layout & Spacing – 8px Base Unit (Locked)

We adopted an 8px atomic unit (Option B) for its balance of granularity and enforceability, with a supplement of 4px for fine adjustments (border radii, letter‑spacing).

5.1 Spacing Scale

All paddings, margins, and gaps are multiples of 8px:

· 0, 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 56, 64, 80, 96, 128, 160, 192.

5.2 Posture‑Specific Spatial Rhythms

Posture Container Width Grid Margins Vertical Rhythm (Line‑height) Padding Modifier
Resting ≤ 1024px 1 or 2 columns Wide (64px desktop) 1.6 (GT Alpina) 2x base (16px) between paragraphs
Walking Forward ≥ 1280px 12 columns, asymmetrical allowed Standard (32px) 1.4 (Inter) 1x base (8px)
Eyes-Forward ≤ 1024px 2 or 3 equal columns Tight (16px) 1.3 (Inter) 0.5x base (4px)
Roaring Calm 1440px+ or full‑bleed 1 or asymmetrical split Expansive (≥ 80px) 1.2 (display) / 1.6 (body) 4x base (32px) between sections

5.3 Breakpoints (Locked)

Breakpoint Device Dominant Posture Rhythm
≤ 480px Mobile Eyes‑Forward (dense, single column); Walking Forward compresses to 1 column; Roaring Calm hero shrinks to 2‑3 lines
481–768px Tablet Walking Forward (2 columns); Resting and Eyes‑Forward remain symmetrical
769–1280px Desktop Roaring Calm and Resting take priority; Walking Forward uses 3‑4 columns
≥ 1281px Large Desktop Full Roaring Calm theatricality; Resting still constrained to ≤ 1024px

Critical rule: On mobile (≤ 480px), Walking Forward colour must be lightened by +10% via CSS filter to maintain legibility against bright outdoor screens.

5.4 Vertical Rhythm Enforcement

We adopt a strict baseline grid – all vertical spacing (line‑height, margin‑bottom, padding) must align to the 8px scale, but each posture has its own ratio (see table). This ensures harmony across pages while respecting each posture’s emotional pacing.

6. Imagery & Photography Style

Each page context inherits the posture’s emotional payload and dictates the photographic treatment.

Posture Lighting & Colour Grade Composition Subject Treatment
Roaring Calm Warm golden hour, high saturation, slight vignette 16:9 or 21:9, car off‑centre, negative space for text Pristine, gleaming, aspirational – drone/wide‑angle
Walking Forward Neutral daylight, even lighting, mid‑saturation 4:3 or 3:2, car centred or angled diagonally, multiple cars in grid Clean but honest, no over‑styling
Eyes-Forward Clinical studio strobes, white background, low saturation, high contrast Square (1:1), straight‑on front/rear/side, extreme close‑ups of VIN, tread, defects Forensic, neutral, all flaws visible – no retouching
Resting Soft diffused light (golden‑hour afterglow or twilight), low contrast, warm neutral 16:9 cropped tight, car low in frame, foreground negative space Peaceful, static, settled – long exposure with slight motion blur in background

Enforcement: Each asset must be tagged with its intended posture; CMS rejects uploads mismatched to page context.

8. Motion & Micro‑Interactions

Motion serves as a transition vehicle between postures and reinforces the emotional shift.

8.1 Easing Curves

Use Case Easing Function Duration
Posture shift (Panther morph) cubic-bezier(0.65, 0, 0.35, 1) (ease‑in‑out) 300ms
Micro‑interaction (hover, expand) cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1) (spring overshoot) 200ms
Page transition cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1) (ease‑out) 400ms
Loading/skeleton linear fade‑in/out 600ms

8.2 Panther SVG Morphing

When the user moves between funnel stages, the Panther path should transition from one posture to the next over 300ms using the ease‑in‑out curve. This requires identical path node counts across postures; if not feasible, use a cross‑fade (opacity transition).

8.3 Hover & Focus States

· Hover: Lightness shift of ±5% on interactive elements (e.g., Roaring Calm button from 50% to 55% lightness).
· Scale: Buttons scale to 1.02 on hover.
· Focus‑visible: 2px ring in Eyes‑Forward colour (#6F767D) with 2px offset – never rely on outline: none.

8.4 Loading States

Skeleton cards use Walking Forward colour at 20% and 60% opacity with a shimmer gradient.

· Border‑radius: 8px (1 base unit)
· Padding: 16px (2 units)
· Hover: shadow 0 8px 24px rgba(0,0,0,0.1), scale 1.01

· Header: fixed top, white background, bottom border 1px solid --panther-eyes-hex at 20% opacity.
· Active link: underline in --panther-roaring-hex (2px) for Roaring Calm pages; use page‑specific colour otherwise.
· Dropdown menus: Walking Forward colours, hovered item shifts to Roaring Calm (indicating action leads to decision).

9.5 Modals & Overlays

· Backdrop: rgba(0,0,0,0.5)
· Modal: White, max‑width 600px, padding 32px (4 units), border‑radius 12px (1.5 units)
· Close icon: top‑right, --panther-eyes-hex turns to --panther-roaring-hex on hover.

11. Accessibility – WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

· Contrast: All colour pairs pass (verified earlier – Resting text on white = 13:1).
· Focus indicators: 2px ring with 2px offset in --panther-eyes-hex on all interactive elements.
· Motion reduction: Respect prefers-reduced-motion – set all durations to 0ms or provide toggle.
· Screen reader: Icons are aria-hidden="true" if decorative; functional icons have accessible labels.
· Colour blindness: Never rely solely on colour – badges include icons, text, and borders.

---

12. Theming (Light vs. Dark)

Default theme: Light – used for all transactional pages (inspection, checkout) where legibility and trust are paramount.

Dark theme – restricted to marketing/hero pages (Roaring Calm zone) only. On dark background (#1A1A1A), Resting becomes invisible, so it is excluded from dark theme. Roaring Calm, Walking, and Eyes‑Forward can appear on dark with a light halo for legibility.

13. Voice & Tone Guidelines

13.1 Invariant Brand Voice (Panther)

· Direct, not diplomatic – no jargon, plain declarative sentences.
· Guardian‑like, not predatory – frames every statement as a reveal of truth, not a hunt for a deal.
· Calmly authoritative – never shouts (no all‑caps), never pleads; states facts with settled confidence.
· Culturally fluent, not exotic – universal or contextually Nigerian without stereotype.

13.2 Tone Modulations per Posture

Posture Tone Descriptor Copy Principle Example
Roaring Calm Sovereign Invitation Expansive, aspirational, declarative. Uses “we” confidently. “We don't sell cars. We reveal the truth about them. The rest is just driving.”
Walking Forward Guided Clarity Neutral, instructive, scannable. Uses imperatives sparingly. “Compare mileage, service history, and price side‑by‑side. No surprises.”
Eyes-Forward Forensic Neutrality Clinical, data‑driven, objective. Removes subjective adjectives. Passive voice where needed. “VIN verified. Service records attached. Odometer reading confirmed at inspection.”
Resting Grounded Reassurance Warm, minimal, terminal. Uses “you” and “your” to centre the user. “You've made your choice. We'll secure the rest. Confirm your details below.”

13.3 CTA Micro‑Copy

Context Phrase Rationale
Roaring Calm (Hero) “Begin” Implies personal journey, not just transaction.
Walking Forward (Search) “Refine” Puts control in user’s hands.
Eyes-Forward (History) “View Report” Forensic and official.
Resting (Checkout) “Secure Deal” Anchors to trust; “Deal” is transactional but not aggressive.

13.4 Enforcement

· All copy must pass the “Roaring Calm” test: read aloud; if it sounds like a nervous salesperson or an AI‑generated blog, it fails.
· Tone linting: CMS tags each text block with its intended posture; a script flags subjective adjectives (e.g., “amazing,” “stunning”) in Eyes‑Forward zones – they must be stripped.
· The word “Panther” is a noun only, never a verb or adjective in body copy (e.g., “Panther‑approved” is forbidden).

14. Naming – Working Name: PANTHER

After evaluating candidate names (ZENITH, VELOS, KARU, etc.) using the naming skill’s methodology, we settled on Panther as the working name. This literal choice unifies the mascot and brand identity into a single, unmistakable signal. Domain preferences: panther.ng, panther.africa, panthercars.ng, or joinpanther.com – availability to be verified.

15.3 Utility Classes (Enforcement Mechanism)

We use utility classes for posture context: .posture-roaring, .posture-walking, .posture-eyes, .posture-resting. These set the colour and font variables automatically. Developers must apply these to containers; linting ensures they are not mixed.

17. Conclusion

The Panther Design System is a complete, internally consistent, and enforceable brand language. Every element – from colour to typography to motion to copy – is derived from the four postures and the core narrative of revealing truth with calm authority. The system is designed to scale across digital and physical touchpoints, to resonate with the target demographic, and to differentiate the marketplace through emotional intelligence and rigorous attention to perceptual detail.

All decisions documented above are locked. Any future deviations must be justified and approved through a formal design governance process.
